About Cheryl Beesley

30+ years of creating beautiful, sustainable landscapes in Texas

Passionate Designer, Skilled Educator

For over three decades, I have been designing and installing landscapes that celebrate the natural beauty of Texas while creating functional, sustainable outdoor spaces for families to enjoy.

My journey in landscape design began with a deep love for plants and a desire to help others connect with nature through their outdoor spaces. What started as a passion has evolved into a comprehensive practice that emphasizes sustainable design principles, native plant communities, and edible landscaping.

Through Adelante Landscapes, I focus on creating gardens that are not just beautiful, but also environmentally responsible, water-wise, and productive. Every project is an opportunity to demonstrate how thoughtful design can enhance both our lives and our local ecosystems.

Design Philosophy

"I believe that every landscape should tell a story - the story of the people who live there, the story of the place, and the story of how we can live in harmony with the natural world."

My approach to landscape design is rooted in the belief that outdoor spaces should be:

  • Sustainable: Working with natural systems and local climate to create landscapes that thrive with minimal inputs
  • Functional: Meeting the real needs of families while creating spaces for relaxation, entertainment, and connection
  • Beautiful: Celebrating the inherent beauty of Texas landscapes while expressing each client's personal style
  • Educational: Demonstrating how thoughtful design can benefit both people and environment
  • Evolving: Creating dynamic landscapes that change and improve over time, reflecting the natural cycles of growth and renewal

Beyond the Business

When I'm not designing landscapes for clients, you'll find me in my own garden, experimenting with new plant combinations, trying out sustainable growing techniques, or simply enjoying the peace that comes from being surrounded by growing things.

I'm passionate about sharing knowledge and regularly speak at local garden clubs, lead workshops on edible landscaping, and mentor new gardeners who are just beginning their journey with plants.

My own garden serves as both a testing ground for new ideas and a personal sanctuary where I can practice what I preach about sustainable, beautiful, and productive landscapes.

What Drives Me

Every project is an opportunity to create something beautiful while making a positive impact on our environment. I love seeing families enjoy fresh vegetables from their own yards, watching children discover the wonder of growing things, and knowing that each landscape I create supports local ecosystems and wildlife.
