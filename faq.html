What plants work best in North Texas clay soil?

Native Texas plants like Texas Red Oak, Flame Leaf Sumac, and native grasses thrive in our clay soil. For edible gardens, we recommend raised beds with amended soil for vegetables, while fruit trees like pomegranates and figs adapt well to clay.

When is the best time to plant in the Dallas area?

Spring (March-May) and fall (September-November) are ideal for most planting in North Texas. Summer heat is too stressful for new plants, while winter limits root development. Our timing guides are specific to DFW weather patterns.

Do you install edible gardens in Dallas?

Yes! We specialize in edible landscaping throughout North Texas. Our edible gardens include vegetables, herbs, fruit trees, and berry bushes designed to be both productive and beautiful. We handle everything from design to installation.

How do you design for North Texas heat and drought?

We use drought-tolerant native plants, efficient irrigation systems, mulching, and strategic shade placement. Our sustainable designs reduce water usage by 30-50% while creating landscapes that thrive in Texas heat.
